Stories

Real people, real loans, real friendships preserved.

M
Software Engineer

My housemate needed $600 for a car repair, engine problem, couldn't wait. I transferred it the same afternoon. I'm not sentimental about money. I just wanted my friend to get to work.

What I didn't want was to become the person who had to ask about it. That's exhausting for everyone. So we put it on Vony: $150 a month, four payments, automatic reminders on his end. He paid on schedule every time. I never once had to mention it, and he never had to feel like he was being chased. That's a better outcome than most informal loans get.

R
Graduate Student

I really struggled to ask. My friend is generous and she would never make me feel bad about it, but I still sat with the text drafted for two days before I sent it. $500 for a tuition shortfall isn't nothing. She said yes immediately, and then somehow that made me feel worse, like I owed her more than just money.

Vony helped me make it concrete. Three payments, proper schedule, reminders that didn't come from me. The moment there was a structure to it, the emotional weight lifted. I stopped treating every coffee we had like a chance to over-explain myself. She got paid back. I stopped feeling guilty. The friendship stayed intact, which was the whole point.

J
Undergraduate

Honestly I didn't think much of it at the time. My coursemate needed $300 for textbooks, I had it spare, so I lent it. We're in the same study group, it felt like the obvious thing to do.

The part I hadn't thought through was what comes next. I'm not the type to bring money up and neither is she, so I was just sort of... hoping it would resolve itself. A mate suggested Vony. We set it up in about five minutes, two payments over two months. The reminders went to her, the money came back, and we never had to have an awkward conversation about it. Term ended fine. We're already planning the same modules next year.
